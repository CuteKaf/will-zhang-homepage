About Us

Frontier Intelligent Robotics Lab (FIR Lab) develops intelligent robotic systems for human-robot collaboration, XR-enabled interaction, robot learning, manipulation, perception, and intelligent manufacturing. Our research aims to make robots more capable partners in complex physical work by connecting perception, reasoning, interaction, learning, and execution. The lab builds methods and systems for robot teaching, collaborative assembly, multimodal interfaces, and intelligent manufacturing environments.

Research Focus

Human-robot collaboration, XR, robot learning, manipulation, perception, and intelligent manufacturing FIR Lab develops intelligent robotic systems that support more effective collaboration between people and robots through perception, reasoning, interaction, learning, and physical execution.

Research Directions

More >
Research directions include: (1) Human-robot collaboration and embodied intelligence; (2) XR-enabled and multimodal interfaces for robot teaching and supervision; (3) Robot learning, manipulation, and long-horizon task execution; (4) Perception, depth completion, and robust system integration for intelligent manufacturing.
